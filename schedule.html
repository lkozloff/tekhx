Slam-Dunks (20min)

Using technology to keep your classroom organised

Amanda Beekman (CISPP)

This slam dunk presentation will cover provide a number of different programs, apps and technologies on how you can stay organised and in your classroom and save time dong it. Get rid of that paper and pencils and go digital!

Doctopus and Goobric (Integration with Google Classroom)

Ben Devadoss (LIS)

Basic information on how to integrate Doctopus and Goobric alongside Google Classroom to manage assignment submission and grading with electronic rubrics.

Tips for Using Google Classroom

Dalton Flanagan (NISC)

Google Classroom is a helpful platform for assigning work, starting online discussions, providing learning tools and linking content. I will give you some tips for implementing it in the classroom.

Youtube

Jan K. (Liger)

This will be a quick introduction on how to set up a Youtube account and upload videos so you can share them with select people, or the world.

Audioslideshows Made Easy With Audacity And Movie Maker

Julien Mariani (LFRD)

Recording your students' voices can be a great way for you to assess them and for them to gain motivation and to improve their skills in various areas (telling, reading, reciting...) Then, they can present nicely what they recorded with their own illustrations thanks to an audioslideshow, which is less time-consuming to produce than a video and will be a rewarding memory of their work. I will provide modest examples produced in first grade, and give a few tips for the simple use of Audacity (audio editor) and Windows Movie Maker (video editor).

Facilitation Self Organizing Teams

Ken Burcham (LIS)

At Logos, we are experimenting with "self-organizing teams" to accomplish the Logos Yearbook and Newspaper. What can you do with Scratch?

Ken Burcham (LIS)

Scratch is a simple, graphical programming environment designed to teach basic programming concepts. See a demo of Scratch, hear how Logos used it in our Programming class and see the projects the students created as their final project.

3D Printing in Education

Ki How Tran (ARC Hub)

I will be presenting some ways to incorporate 3D printing into education and also talk a bit about the work we've already done with NGO's here in Cambodia

Open Source Software You Should Know

Lyle Kozloff (Asian Hope)

There are many amazing, community created free software packages that teachers need to know about! Software packages discussed include early elementary ed, astronomy, digital art, document layout and music notation.

Quick Tips for Chrome

Lyle Kozloff (Asian Hope)

Useful tips for getting the most out of your Chrome browser. Work more efficiently in a tool you use every day.

GitHub for Educators

Lyle Kozloff (Asian Hope)

Github as an alternative to traditional LMSes is quickly becoming not just viable, but preferable. Learn about a new (launched Sept 23) product Classroom for Github may transform the way you have students do assignments.

Libraries, social media and website curation

Libraries are about information dissemination... this means making information as accessible as possible and making sure that your patrons know what is available - or happening in the library / literature world! Social Media, book marking, website curation...

Topics (60min)

Inquriy/Experiential Based Learning

Jeff Holte (Liger)

Liger Learning Center's approach to pedagogy is somewhat unique in its inquiry/experiential based approach. Jeff will introduce Liger's approach, highlight some of the successes and invite the particpants to share their own ideas and experiences in this discussion group.

Learning Outside the Classroom with Technology

Jeff Holte (Liger)

Looking for ideas about how technology can be used by your students creatively and effectively when they are not in class? This session is for anyone wanting to enhance their teaching by having their students use technology to improve learning outside the walls of their classrooms. Examples include: students using phones, cameras etc. to create image and videos (digital stories), using GPS to map to map the community, using tablets for sound recording, note taking, looking at stars, and more.

iMovie

Karine Dallaire (CISPP)

iMovie is an incredible program to use in the classroom. You can have an easy access to a movie studio that a few years ago was available only for the experts. In this hour session, you will learn the basics by creating collectively a little movie. You do need a Mac computer to assist this session.

Google Apps: 20 Tips in 60 minutes

Lyle Kozloff (Asian Hope)

Get the most out of Google with these 20 tips, both advanced Google users and beginners are welcome!

Digital Story Telling with Explain Everything!

Matt Dolmont (ISPP)

Using the virtual whiteboard application Explain Everything! (for iOS, Android, and Chrome OS), learn to create, edit and combine images, voice narration and video elements to tell animated stories. With the elementary student in mind, we will explore ways to use existing tools to redefine student storytelling with suggestions on how best to curate and share completed work. Bring your tablet to create your own!

ICT in Science Lessons

Piotr Mazowiecki-Kocyk (NISC)

The virtual world offers a variety of opportunities to facilitate learning and engage students into scientific concepts. The presentation will focus on ICT tools that can be used during science lessons and make them both thought provoking and entertaining. We will explore tools such as: Jing, TEDed, PhET and online labs.The meeting is addressed to all teachers keen on science and/or working at secondary school.

BYOD: Examining the Pros & Cons of Technology

Vanessa Gomez (CISPP)

n this discussion, we will talk about the use of classroom technology as a tool for the 21st century learner as well as the health & societal implications of increased screen time. Work, play and socialisation are increasingly done through various technological mediums. Do we feel positive, negative or neutral about this shift? What does it mean for the future? What is our role as educators?

Transforming The Classroom (WORKSHOP)

Wesley Przybylski(Bangkok - Thai-Chinese International School)

In this workshop we will be looking into 3 learning goals that redefine the learning experience and have fun with the tech tools that support them.
